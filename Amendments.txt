1. 100th Constitutional amendment :
The 100th Amendment of the Indian Constitution passed in 2015, established the exchange of some territories between Bangladesh and India. It approved the 1974 Land Boundary Agreement (and its 2011 protocol), which resulted in the exchange of enclaves and the retention of property.


2. 7th amendment 1956 :
	a)7th Constitutional Amendment Act of 1956 promoted the appointment of the same person as a governor for two or more states.
	b)It is related to the reorganisation of states 
	c)7th Constitutional Amendment also provided for the appointment of additional and acting judges of the high court. 
	d)It has provided for the establishment of a common high court for two or more states



3. 10th amendment 1961 :
Incorporation of Dadra, Nagar and Haveli as a union territory, consequent to acquisition from portugal.


4. 12th amendment 1962 :
	a)Goa, Daman and Diu were constituted as a union territory by the 12th Constitutional Amendment Act, 1962.
	b)Later, in 1987, Goa was conferred a statehood. Consequently, Daman and Diu was made a separate union territory.

5. 14th amendment 1962 :
Puducherry, karaikal, mahe and yanam  were made a union territory by the 14th Constitutional Amendment Act.


6. 15th amendment 1963:
​Raise the retirement age of High Court judges from 60 to 62.


7. 26th amendment 1971 :
Abolition of privy purse paid to former rulers of princely states.


8. 38th amendment :
38th Amendment Act inserted a new clause (4) in Article 123 stating that the President’s satisfaction while promulgating an Ordinance was final and could not be questioned in any court on any ground. however, the 44th Amendment to Indian Constitution reversed it and made the President’s satisfaction to bring ordinance justiciable.


9. 1st Amendment 1951:
	a) Empowered the state to make special provisions for the advancement of socially and economically backward classes.
	b) Added Ninth Schedule to protect the land reforms and other laws included in it from the judicial review. After Article 31, Articles 31A and 31B were inserted.
	c)Added three more grounds of restrictions on freedom of speech and expression: public order, friendly relations with foreign states and incitement to an offence. Also, it made the 	  restrictions ‘reasonable’ and thus, justiciable in nature.



10. 22nd constitutional amendment 1969 :
the 22nd Constitutional Amendment Act (1969) created Meghalaya as an ‘autonomous state’ or ‘sub-state’ within the state of Assam with its own legislature and council of ministers



11. 35th amendment 1974 :
This amendment introduced a new class of statehood under the constitution by conferring on Sikkim the status of an ‘associate state’ of the Indian Union


12. 36th amendment 1975 :
enacted to make Sikkim a full-fledged state of the Indian Union (the 22nd state). This amendment amended the First and the Fourth Schedules to the Constitution and added a new Article 371-F to provide for certain special provisions with respect to the administration of Sikkim. It also repealed Article 2-A and the 10th Schedule that were added by the 35th Amendment Act of 1974.
